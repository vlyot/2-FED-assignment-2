Timeline of Covid-19 in its peak, from January 2020 to January 2021

January 2020

Cluster of pneumonia cases identified in Wuhan, China, later confirmed as a novel coronavirus (SARS-CoV-2). First confirmed case outside China reported in a traveler returning to the US. Human-to-human transmission confirmed.

Febuary 2020

Rapid Spread in China: The virus spreads rapidly within China, leading to the lockdown of Wuhan and other cities. Lockdown in Wuhan, China WHO Declares PHEIC: The World Health Organization (WHO) declares a Public Health Emergency of International Concern (PHEIC).

March 2020

Pandemic Declaration: The WHO declares COVID-19 a pandemic, signifying its widespread global presence. Lockdowns and Social Distancing: Many countries implement strict lockdown and social distancing measures to contain the virus. Healthcare Systems Overwhelmed: Healthcare systems in some regions become overwhelmed due to the surge in cases.

April 2020

Peak of First Wave: The first wave of infections peaks in many countries due to lockdown measures and increased awareness. Economic Disruption: The pandemic causes significant economic disruptions and societal changes worldwide. Research Intensifies: Research on the virus, diagnostics, and treatment options intensifies..

May 2020

Easing of Lockdowns: Some countries begin to gradually ease lockdown measures, leading to a cautious reopening of societies. Continued Vigilance: Concerns remain about the potential for a resurgence of cases. Focus on Recovery: Efforts shift towards economic recovery and supporting impacted communities.

June 2020

Easing of Lockdowns: Some countries begin to gradually ease lockdown measures, leading to a cautious reopening of societies. Continued Vigilance: Concerns remain about the potential for a resurgence of cases. Focus on Recovery: Efforts shift towards economic recovery and supporting impacted communities.

July 2020

Resurgence of Cases: Some regions experience a resurgence of cases, prompting renewed public health measures. Global Cooperation: International cooperation on research, development, and resource sharing increases. Focus on Equitable Access: Concerns arise about ensuring equitable access to vaccines and other resources for all countries.

August 2020

Continued Challenges: The pandemic continues to pose significant challenges for healthcare systems, economies, and societies worldwide. Vaccine Trials Advance: Vaccine trials advance, with some candidates entering Phase 3 trials. Mental Health Concerns: Mental health concerns associated with the pandemic become increasingly recognized.

September 2020

School Reopenings: Schools in many countries begin to reopen, with safety measures in place. Testing and Tracing Efforts: Testing and tracing efforts are ramped up to identify and isolate cases. Economic Impact Deepens: The economic impact of the pandemic continues to deepen, with job losses and business closures.

October 2020

Second Wave Peaks: The second wave of infections peaks in some countries, but concerns remain about ongoing transmission. Vaccine Hopes Rise: Positive results from late-stage vaccine trials raise hopes for a potential end to the pandemic. Fatigue and Misinformation: Concerns grow about pandemic fatigue and the spread of misinformation.

November 2020

Vaccines Receive Emergency Use Authorization: The first COVID-19 vaccines receive emergency use authorization in some countries, marking a significant turning point. Vaccination Campaigns Begin: Vaccination campaigns begin in some countries, prioritizing healthcare workers and high-risk groups. Holiday Season Concerns: Concerns arise about the potential for increased transmission during the holiday season.

December 2020

Continued Vaccine Rollout: Vaccination campaigns continue to expand, offering hope for controlling the pandemic. However, vaccine availability and distribution remain uneven globally. Holiday Surge: Concerns about increased transmission during the holiday season materialize, leading to a rise in cases in many countries. New Variants Spread: The B.1.1.7 variant (later known as Alpha) becomes dominant in many regions, raising concerns about its increased transmissibility and potential to reduce vaccine effectiveness.

January 2021

Peak of First Wave: Due to a combination of lockdown measures, increased awareness, and the initial impact of vaccinations, the first wave of infections globally reaches its peak in January. However, significant regional variations exist. New Lockdowns and Restrictions: The emergence of new variants and the holiday surge prompt some countries to reimpose stricter lockdown measures and travel restrictions. Focus on Vaccination: Vaccination campaigns become the primary focus of pandemic control efforts, aiming to achieve widespread immunity and prevent further waves.
